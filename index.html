About Me

I was born and raised in a small but beautiful city in southwest Iran with stunning landscapes in the suburbs. Since I was a child, I knew I wanted to be an artist, but there was no art school in my hometown, so I had to pursue my education in another field. After graduation, I was determined to apply for a bachelor’s degree in fine arts and ranked 600 in the national entrance exam. When I entered the art university, I started my transformation journey by observing the works of well-known artists, and my professors made me more enthusiastic about working hard to develop my knowledge in that area. In the university, I was fortunate to paint vis a vis not only my classmates but also my teachers, which increased my proficiency. Furthermore, holding group exhibitions on campus by various students with different mental attitudes opened my eyes to thinking outside the box. After receiving my bachelor’s degree, besides working in my private studio, I started my career as an art teacher in art schools and colleges to positively influence my students. I worked daily to pass everything I had learned to my students. Most importantly, I attempted to remind them of the importance of art in our lives and how humankind has struggled to beautify their world with art, soothe their pain and make themselves perfect. Moreover, I encouraged my students to create artwork and follow their standards by setting up small exhibitions at the schoolyard. Indeed, working as a teacher improved my soft and technical skills as schoolchildren had different capabilities, which needed a variety of teaching methods to nurture their creativity. When my son was born, I was worried about not painting anymore because of household chores and parental responsibilities. Still, my passion for painting was far more, which gave me the power to balance my personal and professional life as I have held some painting exhibitions and workshops since then.
